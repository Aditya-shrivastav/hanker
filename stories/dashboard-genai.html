Great dashboards are powerful. They centralize metrics, visualize trends, and help organizations monitor performance.

But a question we often ask our customers is: What if you could talk to your dashboards?

At Source Node Technologies, we believe meaningful innovation starts with listening - not just to data, but to the people using it. We take the time to deeply understand our customers journeys, their expectations, and the gaps that quietly emerge even after significant technology investments.

The Challenge

One such gap surfaced during our engagement with an enterprise client that had invested heavily in analytics. Over time, they had built 100+ Power BI dashboards, each carefully designed to serve different teams and business functions.

The dashboards were robust. The data was structured. The metrics were trusted.

Yet, when the organization began exploring a GenAI-powered conversational experience, they hit a wall.

Despite having rich, structured data, the dashboards could not be directly leveraged by large language models in a reliable and performant way.

The result was a familiar enterprise dilemma:

  • Dashboards answered predefined questions
  • Executives wanted conversational, exploratory insights
  • Rebuilding analytics from scratch was not an option

Simply exposing raw data to an LLM was neither safe nor scalable. And recreating business logic outside Power BI would break trust in existing reporting.

Our approach focused on unlocking value from existing investments - not replacing them.

1. Translating Dashboards into Semantic Intelligence

Power BI dashboards are driven by DAX expressions that encode deep business logic. However, DAX is not natively understandable by LLMs.

We solved this by implementing a DAX-to-semantic conversion layer - translating complex calculations, relationships, and measures into a semantic representation that large language models can reason over accurately.

This ensured that when the GenAI system responded to a query, it was grounded in the same logic users already trusted inside their dashboards.

2. Designing a Multi-LLM Architecture

Not all GenAI tasks are the same - and expecting a single model to handle everything often leads to poor performance and high costs.

We designed a multi-LLM architecture where:

  • One model focused on intent understanding
  • Another handled semantic reasoning over structured data
  • A response model ensured clarity, accuracy, and context

This separation of responsibility allowed the system to remain fast, reliable, and scalable - even when interacting with complex analytical queries.

The Outcome

The result was a GenAI-powered conversational bot that could:

  • Answer natural language questions over Power BI data
  • Respect existing business logic and definitions
  • Deliver fast, context-aware responses
  • Operate without compromising performance or reliability
Most importantly, the solution unlocked the full potential of existing analytics investments - transforming dashboards from static visuals into interactive conversations.
